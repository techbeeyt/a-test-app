Instructions

You have been contacted via the Amazon MTurk platform to invite you to participate in our study investigating the effect of user comments or replies on the trustworthiness of Twitter posts.
To successfully complete the HIT, you will be required to complete TWO tasks.
Please attempt this survey on a laptop or a desktop computer.


Tasks

Task 1: You will be required to analyse 28 recent Twitter posts, each containing a news article. You will be asked to provide your opinion on the trustworthiness of each post, and rate how likely you are to like, comment or reply, fact check, share (retweet) or report it, both before and after seeing user comments or replies (STRICTLY WITHOUT browsing the Internet). This process will be repeated 28 times, and is estimated to take up 55 minutes.

IMPORTANT: For the purposes of Task 1, a user comment that criticises an article's credibility is a comment that is CRITICAL of its credibility (i.e., believes the news article is fake news). A comment that does not criticise an article's credibility is a comment that is SUPPORTIVE of the article's credibility (i.e., believes the news article is real). Carefully read through the annotated examples given below to understand the difference between Critical and Supportive comments.



Task 2:Upon successful completion of Task 1, you will be required to complete a 44-item personality questionnaire. This is expected to take 5 minutes to complete.

** You are expected to complete BOTH tasks in one sitting, which will take a maximum of 60 minutes.
** Do NOT refresh the page, or use forward and back controls of the web browser during the experiment. If you do so, your data will be lost and you will not receive any payment.

Task 1
You will see screenshots of several Twitter posts.
Imagine that they appear on your news feed as shared by a distant friend.
Read the posts carefully and,
Indicate your familiarity with the Twitter post.
Rate the trustworthiness of the post, and provide your confidence on the rating.
Indicate how likely you are to like, share, comment or reply, fact check or report the post.
See how other Twitter users have commented on the post.
After reading their comments or replies, identify how others feel about the trustworthiness of the post.
